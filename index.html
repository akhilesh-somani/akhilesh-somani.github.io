Predicting transgene expressions in genetically engineered crops
Data Science Intern
Corteva Agriscience

I worked on predicting transgene expressions in genetically engineered crops (corn and soy) using machine learning. I was successful in achieving prediction r^2 scores of up to 0.75 (considered good prediction accuracy in a field like biology) by implementing, tuning and comparing performances of various statistical tools (linear & polynomial regression, regularization methods) and machine learning models (neural networks, decision trees, ensemble methods, K-nearest neighbors). I also built an interactive R-Shiny web application as a GUI for biologists to assist them before conducting experiments, thus resulting in monetary & time savings. During my internship, I was exposed to Kubernetes which I then used to run the deep learning models on the GPU cluster for faster execution. I ideated and implemented numerous strategies to refine and transform data to accurately capture underlying biological phenomena. I also performed cluster analysis, principle component analysis (PCA), & Naive Bayes classification, for exploratory data analysis and gaining useful insights from the data.

(1) Autonomous Vehicle Safety Analysis:
I analyzed data for autonomous vehicle (AV) testings done in California to gain insights into the AI-control of AVs using statistical and probabilistic approaches. Computational Modeling and Optimization of Slithering Snake
University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

We modeled the underlying physics, and simulated and visualized the optimized motion of a slithering snake in Python. I coupled the differential equations governing the physics of the snake, and performed time and spatial discretization to solve for dynamics and kinematics of the snake. I coded and implemented Genetic Algorithm and Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy to maximize the forward velocity of the snake by optimizing activation torque generated by the snake's muscles and analyzed numerous multi-minima functions like Griewank, Bohachevsky, Rastrigin.

Data Analysis of Nanoscale Thermal Protrusions on Hard-Disk Drives
Summer Intern
Computer Mechanics Laboratory, University of California at Berkeley

I conducted experiments on the nanoscale protrusions (~10-50 nm) of Thermal Fly-height Control Resistor (used to decrease fly-height of the head over the disk & also reduce the risk of head - disk contacts) using Zygo Interferometer. I used MATLAB to clean and process the data (captured from the experiments) by performing operations like transformations and noise filtering. I analyzed the data and derived a linear relation between the protrusions and power input to the resistor. I also worked on experimentally determining the thermal protrusions (~5 nm) of the Touchdown Sensor (used in the touchdown detection of the read/write head and the disk) using Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM) and Gwyddion software. Finally, I compared the precision and accuracy of Atomic Force Microscopy & Zygo Interferometer by obtaining and analyzing surface roughness parameters like arithmetic mean deviation, root mean squared, kurtosis, etc. of a hard disk drive.




Design and Manufacturing of Novel Hybrid Heat Exchanger
Graduate Research Assistant
Sinha Group, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

We are collaborating with 4 research groups and working in a team of over 10 people to develop a novel metal-polymer heat exchanger. There are several aspects to the heat exchanger - thermal, controls, design, manufacturing, fouling, etc. I lead the design and manufacturing team to fabricate and test the heat exchanger. I designed and fabricated an automated adhesive joining setup (using SolidWorks and 3D printing) for implementing metal-polymer bonds in the heat exchanger. I also developed a fundamental mechanics model to explain the underlying physics and tackle issues encountered in the roll-to-roll manufacturing process. I am currently working to demonstrate the working of the heat exchanger under actual industrial conditions.


Model Optimization of Additively Manufactured Hybrid Heat Exchanger
Summer Intern
AHX-PI Lab, University of Maryland at College Park

I optimized the design of Cross-Media Fiber Heat Exchanger (CHX) by performing thermal & structural stress optimizations in ANSYS to minimize cost & weight and maximize heat transfer. I conducted Reliability tests to measure thermal & structural stress endurance limits of the CHX and applied the Arrhenius Model to predict its life under different industrial operating conditions. I determined the best materials for 3D printing the CHX by analyzing stresses in the structure. I compiled over 20 material candidates for wall, coating, and fiber with their essential properties. I also assisted in building an experimental setup to test the CHX by procuring equipment like Pressure Transducers, Air Mixing Chambers, Steam Pressure Regulators, Motor Pumps, Valves, etc.
