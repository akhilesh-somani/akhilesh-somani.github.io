Data Science Projects
University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign
(1) Autonomous Vehicle Safety Analysis:
I analyzed data for autonomous vehicle (AV) testings done in California to gain insights into the AI-control of AVs using statistical and probabilistic approaches. I performed Hypothesis testings and Kolmogorov-Smirnov tests to compare AVs' performance results with human drivers, predicting an accident probability of 213 times higher, implying AVs not yet ready for large-scale deployments. I developed a Naive Bayes Model (from scratch) to predict, with over 80% accuracy, the cause of AV failure under different conditions.
[Github Code] [Presentation]
(2) Unsupervised Stool Sample Analysis in Hepatic Encephalopathy:
I studied stool sample data for liver cirrhosis patients to analyze and predict the microbes responsible for brain damage. I refined and cleaned the raw data by coding and implementing Bayesian Networks on multiple data-sets. I implemented KS tests, Multiple testings, and Q-Q plots to perform statistical analysis on microbe abundance. I performed dimensionality reduction using PCA and clustering using KMeans, GMM, Hierarchical clustering, etc. to successfully identify & study taxonomical relations between 20 microbes (from 150) with altered abundance levels.
[Github Code] [Presentation]
(3) Data Analytics in High-Performance Computing Security:
We analyzed data for security in high-performance computing regime to study the attack sequences and predict compromised states of the system. We parsed the raw data from network packets into analysis-friendly format by using Pyshark. We identified attacker and legitimate user and DNS servers to study the origins and progression of the multi-stage attacks. We also implemented Hidden Markov Models and Factor Graphs to predict the likely state of attacks.

Computational Modeling and Optimization of Slithering Snake
University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

We modeled the underlying physics, and simulated and visualized the optimized motion of a slithering snake in Python. We coupled the differential equations governing the physics of the snake, and performed time and spatial discretization to solve for dynamics and kinematics of the snake. We coded and implemented Genetic Algorithm and Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy to maximize the forward velocity of the snake by optimizing activation torque generated by the snake's muscles and analyzed numerous multi-minima functions like Griewank, Bohachevsky, Rastrigin.

Design and Manufacturing of Novel Hybrid Heat Exchanger
Graduate Research Assistant
Sinha Group, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign

We are collaborating with 4 research groups and working in a team of over 10 people to develop a novel metal-polymer heat exchanger. There are several aspects to the heat exchanger - thermal, controls, design, manufacturing, fouling, etc. I lead the design and manufacturing team to fabricate and test the heat exchanger. I designed and fabricated an automated adhesive joining setup (using SolidWorks and 3D printing) for implementing metal-polymer bonds in the heat exchanger. I also developed a fundamental mechanics model to explain the underlying physics and tackle issues encountered in the roll-to-roll manufacturing process. I am currently working to demonstrate the working of the heat exchanger under actual industrial conditions.
